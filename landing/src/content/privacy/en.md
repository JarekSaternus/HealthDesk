## 4. Cookies

### 4.1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on the User's device when visiting the Website. They are used to remember preferences, analyse traffic, and improve the User experience.

### 4.2. Types of Cookies Used

**Strictly Necessary Cookies**
Enable the proper functioning of the Website. They do not require User consent. These include session cookies and cookies that remember consent choices.

**Analytics and Statistics Cookies**
Used to collect information about how the Website is used. They require User consent. These include cookies from the tools described in section 4.3.

### 4.3. Analytics Tools Used on the Website

**Google Analytics (Google LLC)**
Used for website traffic analysis. Google Analytics uses cookies to collect data about how the Website is used. Data may be transferred to Google servers in the United States. More information: [policies.google.com/privacy](https://policies.google.com/privacy). Opt out: [tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout](https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout).

**Google Tag Manager (Google LLC)**
A tool for managing tags (scripts) on the website. Google Tag Manager itself does not collect personal data, but it manages deployed scripts that may process such data. More information: [policies.google.com/privacy](https://policies.google.com/privacy).

**Hotjar (Hotjar Ltd.)**
A tool for analysing User behaviour on the website (heatmaps, session recordings, surveys). Hotjar processes data relating to User interactions with the website. Data may be transferred to Hotjar servers in Europe. More information: [hotjar.com/privacy](https://www.hotjar.com/privacy/). Opt out: [hotjar.com/policies/do-not-track](https://www.hotjar.com/policies/do-not-track/).

### 4.4. Managing Cookies

You can change your cookie settings at any time:

- **Cookie banner** — on your first visit, a banner allows you to accept or decline specific cookie categories.
- **Browser settings** — you can manage cookies in your browser settings (delete, block, or allow cookies from selected sites).
- **Opt-out tools** — links to provider-specific opt-out tools are provided in section 4.3.

Restricting cookies may affect certain features of the Website.

## 6. Data Retention

Data collected through cookies is retained for the period specified in the settings of each cookie (from the browser session up to a maximum of 26 months for Google Analytics). After this period, the data is automatically deleted. You can delete cookies from your device at any time.

## 7. User Rights

### 7.1. Rights Under the GDPR (EEA Residents)

Every User whose personal data we process has the right to:

- **Access** their personal data (Art. 15 GDPR),
- **Rectification** of inaccurate data (Art. 16 GDPR),
- **Erasure** — "right to be forgotten" (Art. 17 GDPR),
- **Restriction of processing** (Art. 18 GDPR),
- **Data portability** (Art. 20 GDPR),
- **Object** to processing based on legitimate interest (Art. 21 GDPR),
- **Withdraw consent** at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to withdrawal.

To exercise these rights, please contact the Controller by email.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. In Poland: **President of the Personal Data Protection Office (PUODO)**, ul. Stawki 2, 00-193 Warsaw — [uodo.gov.pl](https://uodo.gov.pl). In the UK: **Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)** — [ico.org.uk](https://ico.org.uk).

### 7.2. Rights of California Residents (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a resident of California (USA), you have additional rights:

- **Right to know** — you may request information about the categories and specific pieces of personal data we have collected.
- **Right to delete** — you may request the deletion of your personal data.
- **Right to opt out of sale/sharing** — **we do not sell or share** Users' personal data as defined by the CCPA/CPRA.
- **Right to non-discrimination** — we will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights.

To exercise these rights, please contact the Controller by email.

### 7.3. Rights of Users from Other Jurisdictions

Users from other countries may have additional rights under local data protection laws (e.g. LGPD in Brazil, PIPA in South Korea, PIPL in China, Privacy Act in Australia). We endeavour to honour these rights to the extent applicable. If you have any questions, please contact us.
